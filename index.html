Key insights

  • Smoking is the single strongest predictor — smokers pay ~4× more ($32,000 vs $7,345 median).
  • BMI and age show positive but modest correlations (0.20 and 0.30 respectively) with charges.
  • Charges are heavily right-skewed; log-transformation was applied before regression to reduce skewness.
  • High BMI combined with smoking creates a distinct high-cost cluster ($20K–$60K range).
  • Age distribution is nearly uniform (18–64), with a slight spike at age 18 (youngest allowed age).
